A book deal is, in itself, a contract between you (the writer) and the publishing house (the publisher). It is legally binding, and it ties both you and your publisher into a deal which means they will publish your book, and in return, you will meet their deadlines and stipulations and you will receive either a royalties and an advance or bjust royalties, no advance. A deal will be agreed usually by your literary agent, and there may be some wrangling between an agent and a commissioning editor or publisher before the deal is finalised at which point, the paperwork will be sent to you as an author to sign.
